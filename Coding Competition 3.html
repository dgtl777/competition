Blizzarder is an unofficial Blizzard website used to learn about Blizzards. It is an all you need to know website for Blizzards.

Fact:
Blizzards are caused by large low-pressure storm systems, clashing with high pressure. The result is very high winds. Moisture from oceans and lakes creates clouds, which grow bigger and higher when the warm air and cold air collide. Combine that with below freezing temperatures and you have a blizzard!
Fact:
Any geographical location that has snowfall can have a blizzard or blizzard like conditions.
Fact:
The term Blizzard was coined by an Iowa newspaper in the 1870s to describe a snowstorm.
The term became widespread and synonymous in the 1880s with dangerous snow storms across the United States and England.


Fact:
Blizzards don't occur suddenly -- they develop over a period of days, and the National Weather Service can predict one several days before it strikes. ... It's important to remember that high winds can also create a "ground blizzard" after a recent snowfall.
Fact:
A severe blizzard is considered to have temperatures near or below 10°F, winds exceeding 45 mph, and visibility reduced by snow to near zero.
Fact:
Blizzards affect people by shutting down cities and transportation systems, damaging property, hurting the economy and causing injuries and loss of life. Blizzards affect the environment by damaging or destroying trees, plants and crops and causing flooding when large amounts of snow melt.
